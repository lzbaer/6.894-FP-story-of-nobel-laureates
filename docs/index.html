Nobel Flowers

What are common conditions and environments that may facilitate scientific creativity?

Let's begin by examining David J. Thouless, a 2016 nobel laureate awarded for his research in "theoretical discoveries of topological phase transitions and topological phases of matter".

The background color represents the type of award.

Thouless was awarded for his contributions in Physics.

The shape of the petal represents the mobility of the laureate. Thouless was born in the United Kingdom and conducted research in the United States. His mobility is X.

The number of petals represents the number of publications each laureate has published, up until receiving the award.

Thouless published X publications, so his flower has Y petals.

Leaves represent collaboration, which we define as whether the laureate shared the prize.

Thouless shared his award with F. Duncan M. Haldane and J. Michael Kosterlitz.

The length of the stem represents age. The longer the stem, the older a laureate was when they received the award.

Thouless was 82 years old when he won the Nobel Prize.
